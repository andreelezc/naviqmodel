Anatomy of a Chart

Learn the common terminology regarding charts to enhance your assessment interpretation.

crop Axes

The horizontal and vertical lines that frame the chart area, typically used to measure and categorize data points.

text_fields Labels

Textual descriptions that provide information about the data, such as axis titles, data series names, and individual data points.

workspaces Data Markers

Symbols or shapes placed at data point locations to represent the value of the data, such as dots on a line chart or bars in a bar chart.

format_list_bulleted Legends

A key that explains the symbols, colors, or patterns used to differentiate data series or categories within the chart.

grid_3x3 Gridlines

Light lines that extend across the chart area, usually aligned with major tick marks, to help read values and compare data points.

edit Annotations

Additional notes or comments added to a chart to explain specific data points or trends, providing context or analysis.

About the Model

Understand the background of our model and learn more about the authors and their research contributions.

The NaVi-Q model is the outcome of a Doctoral Thesis research. Drawing from established models in the field of Software Engineering and Information Visualization, as well as the ISO/IEC 25000 Standards, this model adapts and extends these concepts to meet the specific requirements of narrative visualizations.

The criteria and properties were derived from a Systematic Mapping Study and analysis of the relevant literature. This review ensured that the model's framework is robust and consistent with the gaps identified in the field. The detailed findings and methodologies of this systematic mapping study are documented in the publications listed below.

These academic papers are central to the development of our model. Each one offers detailed insights into the methodologies and findings that contributed to its creation.

  • Data-driven Storytelling to Support Decision Making in Crisis Settings: A Case Study
  • Narrative Visualizations Best Practices and Evaluation: A Systematic Mapping Study
  • NaVi-Q: A Quality Model for Evaluating Narrative Information Visualizations

Since 2018, Andrea has been an active member of the Software Quality Research Group at the university. Her research is centered on narrative visualization, with a particular focus on the evaluation of visualizations.
